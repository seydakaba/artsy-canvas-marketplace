Who was Angelica Kauffmann

Maria Anna Angelica Kauffman (30 October 1741 – 5 November 1807) was a Swiss Neoclassical painter. She is best known as a history painter but Kauffmann was also a skilled portraitist, landscape and decorative painter. She had a successful career in Rome then London. In Rome she was accepted into the Accademia di San Luca at the precocious age of 23 and she was, along with Mary Moser, one of two female painters among the founding members of the Royal Academy in London in 1768.

Kauffman in the Photographic Archives The Paul Mellon Centre Photo Archive consists of mounted reference images depicting works of art. It has been digitised in its entirety, and contains in excess of 400 images associated with Angelica Kauffman. There are a number of self portraits, as well as folders grouping together drawings and engravings after the artist. The Tate Photo Archive has not been digitised, but contains four folders of images associated with Kauffman. It can be accessed on site with an appointment.

Kauffman in the Archive Collections Brinsley Ford Archive Sir (Richard) Brinsley Ford (1908–1999) was a gentleman-scholar, connoisseur and collector. The Brinsley Ford Archive contains material concerning British and Irish travellers in Italy in the eighteenth century, compiled by him between c.1950 and 1988 and later edited for publication by John Ingamells as A Dictionary of British and Irish Travellers in Italy, 1701-1800 (1997). At the heart of the archive sits a large sequence of material – which includes research notes, correspondence, transcriptions and copies of original sources, photocopies of published material, publications and photographs – organised alphabetically by traveller. Within this sequence, there are two files concerning Kauffman. The Brinsley Ford Archive has been catalogued and the description can be searched online.

Ellis Waterhouse Archive Sir Ellis Kirkham Waterhouse (1905–1985) was an art historian and museum director. The Waterhouse Archive contains a series of ‘Research files on artists’ which include the material he created, collected and compiled on different artists throughout his career. Within this series there is one file concerning Kauffman, which includes 80 images of works by the artist. The Ellis Waterhouse Archive has been catalogued and the descriptions can be searched online. The above archive collections contain substantial amounts of material for Kauffman. You may also discover smaller quantities of material, such as individual letters referencing the artist, research notes and photographs, by searching the online catalogue and boxlists.
